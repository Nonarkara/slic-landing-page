§ 01 · The Firm

Not just consultants. Innovation partners.

Smart & Livable City Co., Ltd. began as friends and colleagues who shared one belief — that cities could be made better with the right tools and a little discipline.

01

Purpose-driven

Every engagement begins with a community need. Never technology for its own sake.

02

Proof-based

Data and measurable outcomes guide every design and delivery decision.

03

People-centric

Citizens are the subject of the work, not the audience of it.

04

Practical

Modular, bankable solutions a municipal budget can actually carry.

SLIC bridges public institutions, private enterprises, and academic networks to co-create data-driven, citizen-centric smart cities. We work with mayors, ministries, and operators — not only with vendors.

Our Thai engineers, architects, anthropologists, urban planners and AI specialists are united by one vision: cities that serve the people who live in them, at a price those cities can afford.

§ 02 · Practice

Innovation as a service.

The 4C framework carries an engagement from first conversation to lasting impact — one contract, four discrete modes.

01 / C

Consultation

Strategic advisory for municipalities and private developers — smart infrastructure, urban sustainability, digital transformation.

02 / C

Connecting

Bridges between government agencies, universities, and the private sector so collaboration and scale become possible.

03 / C

Capacity Building

Training smart city officers, community leaders, and local teams in the technical and creative methods they need.

04 / C

Co-Creation

Collaborative implementation through people-public-private partnerships (4Ps) — from idea through evaluation.

§ 05 · LFC · Flagship Programme

Leadership for Change Cohort 16.

A 9-module executive programme for practical smart-city leaders — co-organised with the Right Livelihood Foundation (มูลนิธิสัมมาชีพ). Dr. Non Akaraprasertkul, Course Director.

What LFC is

Now in its sixteenth cohort, LFC has trained over 1,500 leaders across government, private sector, and civil society. This year's theme — Practical Smart City Leaders — is organised around a 4P framework: Purposeful, Practical, Proof-based, People-Centric.

Who should apply

City executives, municipal officers, smart city project managers, SE/SME/startup founders, policy designers, PPP teams, and civil society leaders intent on building real, bankable smart-city projects.
